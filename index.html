About Me

I'm an aspiring computer scientist from Old Lyme, Connecticut, a small shoreline town at the mouth of the Connecticut River. I came to Cornell University in Ithaca, New York, in the summer of 2018, and since my arrival, I've focused on my career in Data Science while enjoying ultimate frisbee on the Shake.

Robotics

For four years, I took on a leadership role on my highschool FIRST robotics team, the Techno-Ticks. As a Hall of Fame team, we had large shoes to fill every year. As the lead programmer, I was responsible for all the code that ran and controlled its movement on the field. As a team leader, I helped to coordinate students and oversee the design, prototyping, and manufacturing stages through the 6-week build season and assumed a continuing role in developing our strategy and incorporating data into our analysis process to gain a better understanding of the game.

Cornell University

I'm a freshman in the Cornell University College of Arts and Sciences, majoring in Computer Science with a vector in Data Science and Analytics. In addition to my coursework, I'm a member of the Cornell Data Science Project team, a student-led organization that works on developing powerful AI-based models and visualizations to analyze and communicate data.
